DESCRIPTION FOR "outside cover reuse"

Image shows an instance of a reused manuscript fragment in binding, where the reuse is for the outside cover of the document. This can be a front cover image or a back cover image. It can even be an image containing the spine, but ONLY IF the outside wrapping is visible and contains text/decoration/illumination on its surface (or visible through its surface). It can also be an image of all three (front cover, back cover, and spine) where some text/decoration/illumination is visible. Once again, if you can see text/decoration/illumination through the cover - i.e. it's on the same material piece, but on the opposite side and visible through the cover surface - this 'O' should be used.
Note that this classification should only be used when the cover is on the book. If it's not on the book, the 'not in situ coVer' classification should be used.

Front-back matter reuse F Front_Matter_Reuse fmr Image shows text from the previous use on one of a couple types of defined folios. First type: the reused document can be on a pastedown - whether it's still pasted down onto the board part of the binding, partially folded back, now unpasted but still directly after the board part of the binding, or included after a new flyleaf or flyleaves with both sides of what was previously the pastedown now available for view. The reused document can also be on a flyleaf. Usually, in an original binding, this is the first folio after the board part of the binding (and usually also the pastedown, which combines with the board to make one block). However, there can be more than one flyleaves used with reuse - corresponding to the first, second, (third, fourth, ...) leaves after the board binding/pastedown block. If the pastedown is unpasted, it can be argued that the pastedown is now a flyleaf, and the original flyleaf becomes a second flyleaf. The important points are: 1) that the pastedown or flyleaf NOT have material that is part of the new codex document nor of the title, description, cataloging, pen trials, etc. for the new codex document; and 2) that the pastedown or flyleaf contain text/decoration/illumination.
